# UK Business Setup Guide

This document covers government support, company formation, and banking options for BettrPrompt.

- 50 year old male, UK resident
- Owns house outright (with wife)
- Wife earns £50k+ per year

## UK Government Support

### Available Support

#### Start Up Loans (Recommended)

Government-backed personal loans for new businesses.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Amount | £500 - £25,000 |
| Interest Rate | 6% fixed |
| Term | 1-5 years |
| Mentoring | 12 months free support included |
| Means Testing | None on household income |

**Website:** [startuploans.co.uk](https://www.startuploans.co.uk)

**Why it suits us:**
- No requirement to be on benefits
- Mentoring valuable for first-time SaaS founders
- £10-15k would cover initial costs (legal, marketing, infrastructure)
- Repayments manageable once revenue starts

#### Help to Grow: Digital

Free government programme for digital adoption.

- 1:1 advice sessions with digital experts
- Help choosing appropriate software/tools
- No cost, no eligibility restrictions
- Useful for validating tech stack choices

#### R&D Tax Credits (Once Trading)

Claim back qualifying research and development expenditure.

| Scheme | Claim Rate | Best For |
|--------|------------|----------|
| SME R&D Relief | Up to 33% | Small companies with R&D costs |
| R&D Expenditure Credit | 20% | Larger companies or grant-funded R&D |

**Qualifying activities for BettrPrompt:**
- n8n workflow development and AI integration
- Personality calibration algorithm development
- Novel prompt optimisation techniques
- Custom framework selection logic

**Recommendation:** Keep detailed records of development time from day one. Even founder time spent on R&D can qualify.

#### SEIS/EIS (For Future Investment)

Tax relief schemes that make your company attractive to investors.

| Scheme | Investor Tax Relief | Max Raise | Time Limit |
|--------|---------------------|-----------|------------|
| SEIS | 50% | £250,000 | First 3 years |
| EIS | 30% | £5 million/year | After SEIS |

**Action:** Apply for SEIS Advance Assurance before seeking investment. This confirms eligibility and reassures investors.

#### Local Enterprise Partnership (LEP)

Regional business support varies by location. Typical offerings:
- Small grants (£500-£5,000)
- Subsidised workspace
- Mentoring programmes
- Networking events

**Action:** Search "[your county] Local Enterprise Partnership business support"

#### British Library Business & IP Centre

Free resources for startups:
- Market research databases (worth £1000s in subscriptions)
- IP advice and trademark searching
- 1:1 business advice sessions
- Workshops on funding, marketing, legal

Available remotely or at regional hub libraries.

### Not Available (Due to Household Income)

| Programme | Reason Not Eligible |
|-----------|---------------------|
| New Enterprise Allowance | Requires Universal Credit eligibility |
| Universal Credit | Household income exceeds threshold (~£30-35k) |
| Jobseeker's Allowance | Household income exceeds threshold |

### Not Recommended Yet

| Programme | Reason to Wait |
|-----------|----------------|
| Innovate UK Grants | Require match funding and significant R&D scope; better once established |
| Growth Hubs | More useful once trading and looking to scale |

## Company Formation

### Recommended Structure: Private Limited Company (Ltd)

A UK Ltd company is the best choice for a SaaS business:

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Limited liability | Personal assets protected |
| Tax efficiency | Corporation tax (25%) lower than higher-rate income tax (40-45%) |
| Credibility | Professional image for B2C/B2B customers |
| Investment ready | Standard structure for SEIS/EIS and future funding |
| Dividend flexibility | Extract profits tax-efficiently |

### Formation Options Comparison

| Provider | Cost | Speed | Includes | Best For |
|----------|------|-------|----------|----------|
| Companies House Direct | £12 (online) | 24-48 hours | Basic incorporation only | DIY with existing knowledge |
| Tide | Free | Same day | Bank account, registered address (£) | Quick setup with banking |
| Starling | Free | Same day | Bank account | Clean interface, established bank |
| 1st Formations | £13-£50 | Same day | Documents, guidance | More hand-holding |
| Rapid Formations | £13-£100 | 2-4 hours | Various packages | Premium features |
| Accountant | £100-£300 | 1-3 days | Professional advice, correct setup | Complex situations |

### Recommended Approach

**Option A: Starling Bank Formation (Recommended)**

1. Apply for Starling Business account
2. Use their free company formation service
3. Receive all documents and bank account together
4. Add accountant later

**Pros:**
- Free formation
- Excellent banking app
- Established, FSCS-protected bank
- Good API for future integrations

**Option B: Accountant-Led Formation**

1. Find a local accountant familiar with tech startups
2. They handle formation, registered office, initial setup
3. Ongoing relationship for tax planning

**Pros:**
- Professional guidance from day one
- Tax-efficient setup (salary vs dividends)
- R&D tax credit expertise
- Costs offset by tax savings

**Recommended if:** Budget allows £150-300/month for ongoing accountancy

### Formation Checklist

- [ ] Choose company name (check availability at Companies House)
- [ ] Decide share structure (typically 100 ordinary shares)
- [ ] Confirm registered office address (home or virtual)
- [ ] Prepare director details (you as sole director initially)
- [ ] Confirm SIC codes: 62012 (Business/software development), 62090 (IT consultancy)
- [ ] Set accounting reference date (typically 31 March or anniversary of formation)

### Post-Formation Tasks

1. **Register for Corporation Tax** - Within 3 months of trading
2. **Open business bank account** - Immediately
3. **Register for VAT** - When turnover approaches £90,000 (or voluntarily earlier)
4. **Set up payroll** - If paying yourself a salary
5. **Get business insurance** - Professional indemnity at minimum
6. **SEIS Advance Assurance** - Before seeking any investment

## Business Bank Accounts

### Comparison for SaaS Startups

| Bank | Monthly Fee | UK Payments | International | Stripe Integration | Best For |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Starling** | Free | Free | Good rates | Excellent | Solo founders, low volume |
| **Tide** | Free | Free | Good rates | Good | Invoicing, expense management |
| **Revolut Business** | Free-£79 | Free | Excellent rates | Excellent | Multi-currency, international |
| **Monzo Business** | Free-£30 | Free | Via TransferWise | Good | Simple needs, personal Monzo users |
| **HSBC Kinetic** | Free 18mo | Free 18mo | Standard rates | Good | Traditional bank credibility |
| **Barclays** | £8/mo | Included | Standard rates | Good | High street presence needed |
| **NatWest/RBS** | £6.50/mo | Included | Standard rates | Good | Free banking periods available |

### Recommended: Starling Business

**Why Starling is ideal for BettrPrompt:**

1. **Truly free** - No monthly fees, no transaction fees
2. **Excellent app** - Clean, fast, reliable
3. **Stripe integration** - Seamless payouts
4. **Multi-currency accounts** - Hold EUR and USD (important for your pricing tiers)
5. **FSCS protected** - UK-regulated, deposits protected to £85k
6. **API access** - Good for future automation
7. **Instant notifications** - Real-time transaction alerts
8. **Free company formation** - If you haven't formed yet

**Limitations:**
- No physical branches
- No cheque deposits (irrelevant for SaaS)
- Limited credit facilities initially

### Alternative: Revolut Business

Consider Revolut if:
- You expect significant EUR/USD transactions
- You want to hold multiple currencies cheaply
- You need virtual cards for services

**Note:** Revolut is not a full UK bank (Lithuanian banking licence). Consider keeping main operating account with Starling and using Revolut for FX.

### Multi-Account Strategy

For a SaaS handling multiple currencies, consider:

| Account | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Starling GBP | Main operating account, Stripe GBP payouts |
| Starling EUR | EUR Stripe payouts (free sub-account) |
| Starling USD | USD Stripe payouts (free sub-account) |
| Revolut (optional) | Ad spend, international subscriptions |

Starling supports holding EUR and USD directly, which simplifies your multi-currency Stripe setup.

## Recommended Action Plan

### Week 1: Foundation
1. Decide on company name
2. Open Starling Business account (includes free formation)
3. Or: Form company via Companies House, then open Starling

### Week 2-4: Setup
1. Register for Corporation Tax (HMRC)
2. Set up accounting software (FreeAgent, Xero, or QuickBooks)
3. Apply for Start Up Loan if needed
4. Get professional indemnity insurance quote

### Month 2: Operations
1. Find an accountant (tech-savvy, R&D tax credit experience)
2. Set up payroll if taking salary
3. Apply for SEIS Advance Assurance
4. Start keeping detailed R&D records

### Before Seeking Investment
1. Ensure SEIS Advance Assurance approved
2. Prepare pitch deck
3. Have 3-6 months of trading history
4. Document all R&D activities
