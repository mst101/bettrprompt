# BettrPrompt Brand & Positioning Guide

*For use in visual design, graphics, and imagery*

---

## Core Value Proposition

### What BettrPrompt does

The Simple Way to Solve Complex Problems: BettrPrompt transforms vague, half-formed ideas into structured AI prompts
that get results - by asking the right questions first.

### The problem we solve

Most people using AI daily get inconsistent results. They know prompting matters but don't know *why* their prompts
sometimes work and sometimes don't. They waste time in frustrating back-and-forth with ChatGPT or Claude, iterating
through three or four attempts before getting something useful. They've heard of "prompt engineering" but don't have
time to study it - they just want better outputs.

### How we solve it differently

1. **We ask before we generate.** Where other tools offer prompt templates or libraries to browse, BettrPrompt actively
   interviews the user - asking targeted clarifying questions to surface what they actually need before producing
   anything. The prompt is the output; the value is the structured thinking process that gets you there.

2. **We select frameworks intelligently.** We analyse the user's task and choose from 60+ proven frameworks (not random
   templates, but established methodologies professionals use). Users see the framework we've selected and a few
   alternatives - enough choice to feel in control, not so much they're paralysed.

3. **We adapt to how you think.** When it matters, BettrPrompt tailors its questions to the user's personality type. An
   analytical thinker and a creative thinker working on the same problem will get different questions - because they
   process differently. We show users *why* we're asking what we're asking, building trust through transparency.

### Positioning statements

**One-line positioning:**

> "BettrPrompt turns vague ideas into AI prompts that actually work - by asking the right questions first."

**Differentiator line:**

> "A prompt tool that adapts to how you think - not just what you're asking."

## Competitive Differentiation

| Competitor Type                                                     | What They Offer                                         | How BettrPrompt Differs                                                                                                      |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Prompt libraries** (PromptBase, etc.)                             | Browse and copy pre-written prompts                     | We generate prompts *for your specific situation* through dialogue, not browsing. No two prompts are the same.               |
| **AI assistants** (ChatGPT, Claude directly)                        | General-purpose chat; user must craft their own prompts | We sit *in front of* the AI - structuring the user's thinking before they ever reach ChatGPT/Claude. We ask what they don't. |
| **Prompt enhancers** (tools that "improve" a prompt you've written) | Take your prompt and make it longer/better              | We start *before* the prompt exists - with a vague idea - and build it through questioning. We don't polish; we construct.   |
| **AI workflow tools** (Zapier, Make)                                | Automate AI into workflows                              | We're focused on the *quality of the prompt itself*, not automation. We're for humans thinking, not systems running.         |

### Our defensible advantage

The personality-aware questioning is our strongest moat. Competitors can build framework libraries (we have 60+, they'll
catch up). Competitors can add clarifying questions (though few do it well). But the combination of framework
selection + personality-adapted questioning + transparency about *why* we're asking what we're asking - that's
genuinely differentiated and hard to replicate without deep product thinking.

## Brand Personality

### Voice characteristics

| Attribute       | What it means                                                                                         | What it's not                                                                 |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Confident**   | We know this works. We don't hedge or apologise. We guide users with quiet authority.                 | Not arrogant, boastful, or salesy. No hype.                                   |
| **Clear**       | Plain language. Short sentences. No jargon unless the user brings it. We explain without patronising. | Not dumbed-down, not over-simplified, not corporate-speak.                    |
| **Warm**        | Friendly, approachable, human. We're a helpful colleague, not a cold tool.                            | Not overly casual, not jokey, not try-hard quirky. No emojis in product copy. |
| **Intelligent** | We respect the user's intelligence. We assume they're capable; they just need structure.              | Not academic, not showing off, not making users feel stupid.                  |
| **Trustworthy** | We're transparent about what we're doing and why. No dark patterns. Privacy matters.                  | Not evasive, not manipulative, not hiding behind fine print.                  |

### Brand personality in one sentence

> BettrPrompt feels like a smart, calm colleague who asks the right questions and helps you think clearly - without
> making you feel like you should have figured it out yourself.

### What we're NOT

- Not playful/quirky (no pirate themes, no mascots, no wacky illustrations)
- Not corporate/enterprise (no stock photos of people in suits pointing at whiteboards)
- Not techy/developer-focused (no code snippets in marketing, no terminal aesthetics)
- Not self-help/guru (no inspirational quotes, no "unlock your potential" language)

### Visual implications

- Clean, uncluttered layouts with breathing room
- Purposeful colour palette (indigo/purple works well - suggests intelligence without coldness)
- Typography that's modern and readable, not decorative
- Illustrations/graphics that feel human and warm but not cartoonish
- Imagery suggesting clarity, structure, focus - perhaps abstract representations of ideas becoming organised, or
  light/clarity emerging from confusion
- If using people, show them in realistic work/life contexts - thinking, conversing, working - not posed stock
  scenarios

## Customer Personas

### Primary Persona: The Competent Pragmatist

*Target now. This is who we're building for first.*

#### Demographics

- Age: 28-50
- Uses AI 5-8 times daily for work
- Professional role: knowledge workers - marketers, analysts, managers, consultants, founders, creatives in commercial
  settings
- Likely paying for ChatGPT Plus or Claude Pro already

#### Psychographics

- Knows prompting matters but hasn't studied "prompt engineering"
- Gets inconsistent results and doesn't always know why
- Values efficiency and "doing things right"
- Appreciates tools that feel personalised, "made for me"
- Willing to pay for something that genuinely works
- Sceptical of hype; needs to experience value before committing

#### Pain points

- Wastes time iterating on prompts that don't quite work
- Frustrated when AI assumes things and runs in the wrong direction
- Feels like they're probably not using AI as well as they could
- Doesn't have time to learn frameworks or prompt engineering theory

#### What they want from BettrPrompt

- Better results, faster
- Confidence that they're "doing it right"
- A tool that understands how they think without requiring effort
- Transparency - show me why this works

#### Emotional outcome

> "Finally, I'm getting consistent results without having to become a prompt expert."

#### Visual/messaging implications

- Show real-world work contexts (laptop, desk, coffee - but not clichÃ©d)
- Emphasise clarity, efficiency, confidence
- Avoid anything that makes them feel like a beginner or out of their depth
- Personality feature should feel like a bonus, not a requirement

---

### Secondary Persona: The AI Enthusiast

*Target later, once core product is proven. These users will find us organically.*

#### Demographics

- Age: 22-40
- Uses AI 10-15+ times daily across multiple tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Midjourney, etc.)
- Often in tech-adjacent roles: developers, product managers, content creators, solopreneurs
- Early adopters; likely tried multiple AI tools already

#### Psychographics

- Already knows about prompt frameworks; may use them inconsistently
- Follows AI news, influencers, and communities (Twitter/X, Reddit, Product Hunt)
- Values optimisation, mastery, staying ahead of the curve
- Likes to tinker and understand how things work
- Status-conscious about being an "AI power user"

#### Pain points

- Wants consistency without manual effort
- Tired of reinventing prompts for similar tasks
- Knows frameworks exist but doesn't always pick the right one
- Wants speed - hates inefficiency

#### What they want from BettrPrompt

- A comprehensive framework library at their fingertips
- Speed and efficiency in prompt generation
- Enough control to feel like an expert, not a passive user
- Something to show off or recommend to others

#### Emotional outcome

> "This is my secret weapon. I'm faster and more consistent than anyone else."

#### Visual/messaging implications

- Can handle more sophisticated language and concepts
- Appreciates seeing the framework names and understanding the logic
- Wants to feel like a power user, not a novice being guided
- Personality feature is a nice differentiator but not the primary hook

---

### Tertiary Persona: The AI-Curious Beginner

*Target later, possibly with a simplified onboarding flow or separate marketing track.*

#### Demographics

- Age: 25-60 (broader range)
- Uses AI 2-4 times weekly; still exploring what's possible
- Non-technical roles: teachers, HR, small business owners, retirees exploring new tools, hobbyists
- May be using free tiers of AI tools; hasn't committed to paid subscriptions

#### Psychographics

- Intimidated by "prompt engineering" - sounds technical and exclusive
- Gets poor results and assumes it's their fault
- Wants to feel smart and capable, not left behind
- Open to guidance but wary of being patronised
- Price-sensitive; needs clear value before paying

#### Pain points

- Doesn't know where to start with AI
- Gets generic or unhelpful responses and gives up
- Feels like AI is "for other people" (techies, younger generation)
- Embarrassed to ask for help or admit confusion

#### What they want from BettrPrompt

- Someone to hold their hand without making them feel stupid
- Confidence that they can use AI effectively
- Results that make them feel capable and modern
- Simplicity above all

#### Emotional outcome

> "I can actually do this. AI isn't as scary as I thought."

#### Visual/messaging implications

- Extremely clear, simple language - no jargon at all
- Reassuring, encouraging tone
- Show relatable, non-techy contexts (home, hobbies, everyday decisions)
- Personality feature is highly appealing - feels personal and safe
- May need separate landing page or onboarding track with even simpler messaging

## Visual Design Implications Summary

| Element               | Guidance                                                                                                                                                                             |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Overall aesthetic** | Clean, modern, calm. Confident but approachable. Smart without being cold.                                                                                                           |
| **Colour palette**    | Current indigo/purple works well. Suggests intelligence, creativity, trustworthiness. Avoid harsh primaries or neon accents.                                                         |
| **Typography**        | Modern sans-serif. Readable, not decorative. Hierarchy should guide the eye clearly.                                                                                                 |
| **Imagery style**     | Abstract representations of clarity/structure emerging from chaos. Light, space, focus. If using people: realistic, diverse, in genuine work/life contexts - not stock-photo staged. |
| **Illustrations**     | If used, should be sophisticated and purposeful - not cutesy or cartoonish. Think "smart diagram" not "fun doodle".                                                                  |
| **Iconography**       | Simple, consistent, functional. Support understanding, don't decorate.                                                                                                               |
| **Avoid**             | Pirate/nautical themes. Robots or AI clichÃ©s (brains, circuits, glowing eyes). Corporate stock photography. Overly playful or quirky elements. Dark/techy terminal aesthetics.      |
