# Comprehensive AI Prompt Engineering Framework Guide

## Introduction

This guide contains detailed information about 57 different AI prompt engineering frameworks to help craft the most
effective prompts. Each framework serves specific purposes and use cases, and understanding which to apply depends on
the context, goals, and complexity of your task.

## Framework Selection Process

Before crafting a prompt, I will ask qualifying questions to understand:

1. **Project Context**: What is the overall goal or purpose?
2. **Output Type**: What format do you need (analysis, content, code, strategy, etc.)?
3. **Complexity Level**: Is this a simple task or complex problem?
4. **Audience**: Who is the target audience?
5. **Constraints**: Are there specific limitations (time, resources, format, length)?
6. **Iterative Need**: Will this require refinement or is it a one-time output?
7. **Decision Factors**: Are you evaluating options or generating ideas?

## Framework Categories

### 1. STRUCTURED CLARITY FRAMEWORKS

*For when you need precise, well-organized outputs*

#### **CRISPE Framework** (Clarity, Relevance, Iteration, Specificity, Parameters, Examples)

- **Best For**: Technical documentation, strategic planning, content creation requiring precision
- **When to Use**: Tasks needing clear structure with defined boundaries
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive coverage, flexible, ensures targeted outputs
- **Weaknesses**: Time-intensive for simple tasks, may limit creativity if over-specified
- **Components**:
    - **Clarity**: Define the task unambiguously
    - **Relevance**: Align with context/audience
    - **Iteration**: Allow refinement through feedback
    - **Specificity**: Add precise details
    - **Parameters**: Set boundaries for output
    - **Examples**: Provide sample formats

#### **RELIC Framework** (Role, Emphasis, Limitation, Information, Challenge)

- **Best For**: Content creation, strategic planning, customer interactions
- **When to Use**: When you need focused, goal-oriented outputs with clear constraints
- **Strengths**: Clear structure, eliminates ambiguity, highly versatile
- **Weaknesses**: Learning curve, requires upfront effort
- **Components**:
    - **Role**: Define AI's role (advisor, creator, analyst)
    - **Emphasis**: Highlight priority areas
    - **Limitation**: Set constraints (word count, scope)
    - **Information**: Provide necessary context
    - **Challenge**: State the goal or problem to solve

#### **RTF Framework** (Request, Task, Format)

- **Best For**: Data retrieval, instructional content, simple clear requests
- **When to Use**: Straightforward tasks with specific format requirements
- **Strengths**: Simple, efficient, clear communication
- **Weaknesses**: Less suitable for complex, exploratory tasks
- **Components**:
    - **Request**: State what you want clearly
    - **Task**: Define what AI should do
    - **Format**: Specify output structure

### 2. ITERATIVE REFINEMENT FRAMEWORKS

*For when outputs need progressive improvement*

#### **RACEF Framework** (Rephrase, Append, Contextualize, Examples, Follow-Up)

- **Best For**: Brainstorming, data analysis, iterative problem-solving
- **When to Use**: Dynamic, exploratory tasks requiring flexibility
- **Strengths**: Highly flexible, encourages iterative refinement, adaptable
- **Weaknesses**: Learning curve, time-intensive for simple tasks
- **Components**:
    - **Rephrase**: Refine wording for clarity
    - **Append**: Add details/constraints
    - **Contextualize**: Provide background information
    - **Examples**: Include sample inputs/outputs
    - **Follow-Up**: Enable iterative improvement

#### **Chain of Destiny Framework** (Iterative Refinement)

- **Best For**: Projects where quality trumps speed, complex ideas
- **When to Use**: When incremental improvements lead to significant quality gains
- **Strengths**: Continuous improvement, customized feedback, versatile
- **Weaknesses**: Time-intensive, depends on quality feedback
- **Process**:
    - Start with baseline prompt
    - Implement feedback loops
    - Provide specific improvement feedback
    - Integrate refinements progressively

### 3. DECISION-MAKING & PRIORITIZATION FRAMEWORKS

*For when you need to evaluate options or prioritize*

#### **RICE Framework** (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort)

- **Best For**: Feature prioritization, marketing campaigns, project selection
- **When to Use**: Need to prioritize multiple options with data-driven approach
- **Strengths**: Quantitative analysis, clear metrics, time-saving
- **Weaknesses**: Requires accurate data, effort estimation can be tricky
- **Formula**: RICE Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort
- **Components**:
    - **Reach**: How many people/systems affected
    - **Impact**: Potential value/improvement
    - **Confidence**: Certainty of success (data-based)
    - **Effort**: Resources required (time, money, people)

#### **SMART Framework** (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)

- **Best For**: Goal-setting, project planning, personal development
- **When to Use**: Setting clear, attainable objectives with defined timelines
- **Strengths**: Goal-oriented, ensures efficiency, widely recognized
- **Weaknesses**: Can be rigid, may limit creative exploration
- **Components**:
    - **Specific**: Clear, precise goals
    - **Measurable**: Quantifiable metrics
    - **Achievable**: Realistic within constraints
    - **Relevant**: Aligned with objectives
    - **Time-bound**: Defined deadline

#### **COAST Framework** (Challenge, Objective, Actions, Strategy, Tactics)

- **Best For**: Project management, strategic planning
- **When to Use**: Systematically breaking down projects aligned with goals
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive, strategic alignment
- **Components**:
    - **Challenge**: Identify the problem
    - **Objective**: Define the goal
    - **Actions**: List required steps
    - **Strategy**: Outline the approach
    - **Tactics**: Detail execution methods

### 4. ANALYTICAL & PROBLEM-SOLVING FRAMEWORKS

*For when you need deep analysis or logical reasoning*

#### **Chain of Thought Framework**

- **Best For**: Mathematical problems, market analysis, scientific explanations
- **When to Use**: Complex problems requiring step-by-step logical analysis
- **Strengths**: Enhances reasoning, promotes depth and clarity, adaptable
- **Weaknesses**: Requires precise prompting, can be time-consuming
- **Structure**:
    - **Introduction**: Present the problem clearly
    - **Breakdown**: Dissect into manageable segments
    - **Logical Progression**: Follow sequential reasoning
    - **Conclusion**: Summarize findings

#### **Tree of Thought Framework**

- **Best For**: Complex problem-solving, scenario planning, brainstorming
- **When to Use**: Exploring multiple possibilities and interconnected outcomes
- **Strengths**: Fosters creativity, strategic thinking, iterative refinement
- **Structure**: Organize prompts into nodes (options), edges (connections), outcomes

#### **FOCUS Framework** (Focus areas for decision-making)

- **Best For**: AI projects requiring precise goal-setting and prioritization
- **When to Use**: Need to concentrate efforts on areas of greatest impact
- **Strengths**: Enhances clarity, maximizes efficiency

#### **Pros and Cons Analysis Framework**

- **Best For**: Decision-making, strategic analysis
- **When to Use**: Evaluating multiple options with benefits and drawbacks
- **Strengths**: Balanced evaluation, comprehensive
- **Application**: Business strategy, policy formulation, personal decisions

### 5. STORYTELLING & NARRATIVE FRAMEWORKS

*For when you need compelling, emotionally resonant content*

#### **BAB Framework** (Before, After, Bridge)

- **Best For**: Marketing copy, case studies, persuasive content
- **When to Use**: Connecting problems to solutions with emotional impact
- **Strengths**: Storytelling approach, emotionally resonant, simple
- **Weaknesses**: May oversimplify complex issues
- **Components**:
    - **Before**: Describe the current problem/challenge
    - **After**: Envision the ideal outcome
    - **Bridge**: Present the solution that gets from Before to After

#### **CAR-PAR-STAR Frameworks**

- **Best For**: Behavioral interviews, performance reviews, personal development
- **When to Use**: Structuring experiences and achievements narratively
- **Variants**:
    - **CAR**: Context, Action, Result
    - **PAR**: Problem, Action, Result
    - **STAR**: Situation, Task, Action, Result

#### **Challenge-Solution-Benefit Framework**

- **Best For**: Marketing, product development, strategic planning
- **When to Use**: Demonstrating clear value and addressing user needs
- **Components**:
    - Identify problem (Challenge)
    - Propose solution (Solution)
    - Highlight advantages (Benefit)

- **Best For**: Education, customer support, simplifying complex concepts
- **When to Use**: Making information accessible to all expertise levels
- **Strengths**: Clarity, simplicity, bridges knowledge gaps

#### **Help Me Understand Framework**

- **Best For**: Educational AI, customer support, simplifying complex information
- **When to Use**: Breaking down intricate subjects for comprehension
- **Strengths**: Enhances learning, user-friendly

#### **TQA Approach** (Thematic, Question, Answer)

- **Best For**: E-learning modules, educational materials
- **When to Use**: Structuring learning and inquiry
- **Structure**:
    - Broad theme
    - Engaging questions
    - Informative answers

## How to Use This Guide

### Step 1: Initial Assessment

When you come to me with a task, I will ask you qualifying questions:

1. **What is your end goal?** (e.g., create content, make a decision, solve a problem, generate ideas)
2. **What type of output do you need?** (e.g., analysis, strategy, creative content, technical solution)
3. **How complex is the task?** (simple/moderate/complex)
4. **Who is the audience?** (technical experts, general public, stakeholders, etc.)
5. **What constraints exist?** (time, format, length, resources)
6. **Do you need iteration?** (one-time or progressive refinement)
7. **Are you evaluating options?** (yes/no)
8. **Do you need creativity or structure?** (or both)

### Step 2: Framework Selection

Based on your answers, I will recommend 1-3 frameworks from the categories above that best fit your needs.

### Step 3: Refinement Questions

Once framework(s) are selected, I'll ask specific questions related to that framework's components to build a
comprehensive prompt.

### Step 4: Prompt Construction

I will construct the prompt using the chosen framework(s), clearly labeling each component.

### Step 5: Iteration (if needed)

For iterative frameworks, I'll guide you through refinement cycles.
